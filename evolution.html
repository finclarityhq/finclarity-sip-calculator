Section 01

The Great
Shift.

From "Saving" in Gold to "Investing" in Bharat. Tracking the 40-year transformation of Indian wealth.

1980 - 2000: The Physical Era

During this period, Indian trust was built on tangibility. Gold was the ultimate insurance against a closed economy. Real Estate was the only perceived way to "multiply" money. Equity was seen as a "Satta" (Gamble) following the Harshad Mehta era.

2000 - 2015: The Digital Awakening

The rise of the NSE, the introduction of DEMAT accounts, and the boom of the IT sector created a new middle class with disposable income. Mutual Funds began their "Sahi Hai" journey, moving the conversation from picking stocks to picking funds.

2020 - Present: The SIP Revolution

Post-2020, India saw a "Retail Explosion." With over 15 Crore Demat accounts and monthly SIP inflows crossing ₹20,000 Crores, the Indian investor is no longer a bystander. We have moved from a nation of savers to a nation of owners.
