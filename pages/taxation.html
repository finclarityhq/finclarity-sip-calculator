Taxation of SIP & Investments in India

Taxation is one of the most overlooked aspects of investing. While investors often focus on returns, the outcome that truly matters is what remains after tax.

In India, investment taxation follows structured rules that vary by asset class and holding period. Understanding these rules helps reduce unexpected liabilities and improves long-term financial discipline.

Basics of Taxation in India

Income earned by individuals in India is taxed either as regular income or as capital gains.

  • Income: Salary, professional income, interest, dividends
  • Capital gains: Profits from selling investments

Capital gains are classified as short-term or long-term based on the holding period.

Taxation of Mutual Funds

Equity Mutual Funds

  • STCG (≤12 months): 15%
  • LTCG (>12 months): 10% above ₹1.25 lakh exemption

Debt Mutual Funds

Post-2023, most debt funds are taxed at slab rates regardless of holding period.

Hybrid Funds

Taxation depends on equity allocation (65% threshold).

Taxation of SIP Investments

Each SIP installment is treated as a separate investment. Holding period is calculated individually for every contribution.

This structure rewards patience and long-term discipline rather than frequent buying and selling.

Dividend Taxation

Dividends are taxed at the investor’s slab rate. TDS may apply beyond specified thresholds.

Other Asset Classes (Educational)

  • Fixed Deposits: Interest taxed annually at slab rate
  • Gold: Capital gains taxation varies by form
  • Real Estate: LTCG after 24 months with indexation
  • Crypto: 30% flat tax under Section 115BBH
