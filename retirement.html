Section 03

The SWP
Pension Killer.

Traditional pensions are dying. The Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) is the institutional answer to lifelong cash flow.

What is an SWP?

An SWP allows you to withdraw a specific amount of money from your mutual fund investments at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly, or annually). Unlike an FD where the principal stays stagnant, in an SWP, the remaining balance continues to participate in market growth.

The Power of Choice

₹1 Cr FD

Gives you ~₹55,000/month. The principal never grows. Inflation eats your value every year.

₹1 Cr SWP

Gives you ₹55,000/month. If the fund grows at 10%, your principal actually increases over time.

The 4% Rule in India

Globally, the "Trinity Study" suggests a 4% withdrawal rate. In India, due to higher inflation (and higher growth), a 5% to 6% initial withdrawal rate is considered safe, provided the portfolio has a 50-60% equity exposure.
