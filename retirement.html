Module 03 // Harvest Mechanics

The Pension
Killer.

Architecting a perpetual cash-flow system that survives inflation, market volatility, and longevity risk.

The traditional Indian retirement was built on the foundation of 'Safety.' Fixed Deposits, Post Office Schemes, and the ubiquitous Life Insurance Annuity. But in 2026, 'Safe' has become a dangerous word. With healthcare inflation running at 12% and lifestyle costs rising, a fixed pension is a slow-motion architectural failure.

I. The Death of the Annuity

An annuity is a contract where you give a massive lump sum to an insurer, and they pay you a fixed 5-6% for life. The catch? The principal is often gone, and that 6% stays 6% forever. In 15 years, your purchasing power is halved. The **Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP)** flips this script.

The SWP Advantage

Equity Participation + Tax Arbitrage

By keeping your core corpus in a growth-oriented fund and withdrawing only a fixed amount, your remaining money continues to compound. Furthermore, SWP withdrawals are taxed as Capital Gains (12.5% LTCG), whereas FD interest is taxed at your income slab (up to 30%+).

II. The Three-Bucket Architecture

Professional retirees don't just 'withdraw.' They architect their corpus into three distinct buckets to mitigate Sequence of Returns Risk:

  • Bucket 1: Immediate (0-2 Years) Parked in Liquid and Overnight funds. This is your "Sleep Well" money. Market crashes don't affect your groceries.
  • Bucket 2: Defensive (3-7 Years) Hybrid and Equity-Savings funds. This bucket refills Bucket 1 and provides moderate growth with low volatility.
  • Bucket 3: Growth (7+ Years) Diversified Equity and Index funds. This is the engine of your wealth that ensures your corpus outlives you.

III. The Math of Sustainability

The global standard is the "4% Rule," but in the Indian context—with 12% equity growth and 6% inflation—a 5.5% Withdrawal Rate is the 'Ultra' sweet spot. This allows your payout to increase annually to match inflation while your principal remains robust.
