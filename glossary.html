50+ Institutional definitions for the modern Indian investor. Moving beyond basic terms into technical architecture.

XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return)

The most accurate measure for SIP returns. Unlike CAGR, which only looks at the start and end, XIRR accounts for the timing of every single installment. In a volatile market, XIRR is your true north.

LTCG (Long Term Capital Gains)

Post-2024, this stands at 12.5% for equity held over 12 months. The first ₹1.25 Lakh is exempt. Understanding the 'Grandfathering' clause is key for pre-2018 investments.

TER (Total Expense Ratio)

The annual fee charged by mutual funds. Even a 0.5% difference in TER can result in a ₹50 Lakh difference in your 30-year corpus. Always prefer Direct Plans to minimize this.

Standard Deviation

A measure of volatility. A fund with high returns but high standard deviation means your journey will be a roller-coaster. Institutional investors look for a 'Sharpe Ratio'—return per unit of risk.

STT (Securities Transaction Tax)

A direct tax levied on every purchase and sale of equities on the exchange. It is the 'toll tax' of the Indian stock market, collected at source by the exchanges.
