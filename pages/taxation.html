Section 02

The 2025 Tax
Architecture.

"Tax is the single largest friction to compounding. To ignore it is to build wealth on a leaky foundation."

1. The New Equity Regime

Following the Union Budget 2024, India has moved to a simplified but higher-tax environment for equity. The distinction between short-term and long-term has been sharpened to discourage speculative trading and reward generational holding.

LTCG (Long Term Capital Gains)

The rate has been increased from 10% to 12.5%.

While the rate is higher, the exemption limit has also been raised. You now pay 0% tax on the first ₹1.25 Lakh of profit per financial year (previously ₹1 Lakh). For a portfolio growing at 12%, this allows for significant tax-free harvesting.

2. Debt Funds: The Slab Rate Trap

One of the most radical shifts for 2025 is the total removal of indexation benefits for Debt Mutual Funds. Regardless of whether you hold a debt fund for 3 years or 30 years, the gains are added to your taxable income and taxed at your Income Tax Slab Rate.

The Risk

Investors in the 30% bracket now pay nearly 1/3rd of their debt returns to the government, making traditional FDs and Debt Funds less attractive than Arbitrage Funds.

The Pivot

Institutional investors are moving toward Equity Savings Funds or Arbitrage Funds, which enjoy Equity taxation (12.5% LTCG) while maintaining a debt-like risk profile.

3. Tax Loss Harvesting

In 2025, professional investors use "Loss Harvesting" to offset the increased LTCG. If you have underperforming stocks (in loss) and winners (in profit), you can sell both. The loss cancels out the gain, reducing your total tax liability to zero.
